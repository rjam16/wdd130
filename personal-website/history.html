History

How It All Started

1940's

In the 1940s, world fairs encouraged millions to demonstrate their ideas and talents.

This included a very rudimentary design for the first electronic game.

Hardly any player could win against the machine, but it displayed the potential for future electronic endeavors.

1950's

Computers could run simulations to play games like chess or tic-tac-toe, but it wasn’t until 1958 that the game “Pong” would be invented.

It was not the first 'video game' but it did bring attention to the idea of one. This game was one of few available on the first gaming console made by Ralph Baer in 1968, 10 years later!

1960's

Computers remained the hub for electronic games.

New computing languages made in colleges like Dartmouth or MIT aided in game creation.

Most creators were still hesitant to broach the visual aspect of game creation, so games followed a style similar to “Choose Your Own Adventure” books.

1970's

This period is most associate with the beginning of the video game industry.

Arcade machinesand home consoles joined the roster of devices one could enjoy a video game.

“Pong” got its ownmachine, the “Atari” brand began, and “Space Invaders” debuted during this decade.

1980's

A majority of classic games were made in this decade, what others might consider the golden age of gaming.

“Pac-Man”, “Donkey Kong”, “Tetris”, and “Zelda” came out during this time.

Nintendo joined the battle of consoles with the “NES” or “Nintendo Entertainment System” and the “Game Boy”.

1990's

The era of gaming began. New games were coming out consistently along with new consoles.

Sega stepped up to bat with Sonic the Hedgehog on the Genesis console along with Sony and their Playstation.

Mortal Kombat enters the arcade market, Blizzard debuts with WarCraft: Orcs and Humans, Zelda adds a new game to its franchise.

2000's

From this point on, gaming skyrockets in quality and quantity.

Games started to branch further in genre. From life simulators like The Sims to music games like Rockband.

Microsoft entered the field with the Xbox and later the Xbox360. Nintendo continues with the DS and the Wii.

The competition between consoles didn’t slow down either. Gaming started to blend with the age of the internet, Steam helped connect more people to computer games.

The mobile game trend also started with games like Angry Birds and Farmville.

2010's

We’ve returned to the modern day. Where games like Minecraft and free-to-play mobile games create new gamers every second.

Quality has expanded to compete even with movie CGI. The quantity so astronomical that it would take a lifetime to play even half of them.

This is the age when gaming became as essential to pop culture as music and film.
